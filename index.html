Oral Ulceration Quiz

1. What is an oral ulcer?

Correct Answer: 2. An oral ulcer is defined as an open sore caused by a break in the mucosal surface of the oral cavity which fails to heal.

2. What is a key characteristic of Behcet's disease?

Correct Answer: 3. Behcet's is a vasculitis that can cause oral ulceration, among other symptoms.

3. Which of the following is a cause of oral ulcers?

Correct Answer: 1. Trauma, such as that caused by ill-fitting dentures, is a common cause of oral ulcers.

4. Which of the following pathogens can cause oral ulceration?

Correct Answer: 4. Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV) can cause oral ulceration.

5. What is a differential diagnosis mnemonic for oral ulceration?

Correct Answer: 2. The mnemonic VITAMIN helps in differential diagnosis of oral ulcers, covering Vascular, Infection, Trauma, Autoimmune, Metabolic, Iatrogenic, Neoplastic.

6. What type of trauma can lead to oral ulceration?

Correct Answer: 1. Physical trauma such as biting the inside of the mouth can lead to oral ulceration.

7. Which autoimmune condition is associated with oral ulcers?

Correct Answer: 3. Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) is an autoimmune condition that can lead to oral ulcers.

8. What type of oral ulceration can be caused by chemotherapy?

Correct Answer: 4. Chemotherapy can cause diffuse superficial ulceration as an adverse side effect.

9. Which medication can cause iatrogenic oral ulcers?

Correct Answer: 3. Bisphosphonates can cause oral ulceration as a side effect.

10. What is a common symptom of oral lichen planus?

Correct Answer: 1. Oral lichen planus often presents as white lacy patches in the mouth.

11. Which oral condition is characterized by repeated trauma from nail picking?

Correct Answer: 2. Repeated trauma from nail picking can cause traumatic oral ulcers.

12. What is a common adverse drug response leading to oral ulceration?

Correct Answer: 3. Bisphosphonates are known to cause oral ulceration as a side effect.

13. Which of the following is NOT typically associated with oral ulceration?

Correct Answer: 4. A high protein diet is not typically associated with oral ulceration.

14. What is the role of Intensity Modulated Radiotherapy (IMRT) in oral ulceration?

Correct Answer: 1. IMRT reduces the incidence of radiation-induced oral ulceration.

15. Which disease is characterized by granulomatous inflammation involving the oral cavity?

Correct Answer: 3. Orofacial granulomatosis (OFG) is characterized by granulomatous inflammation involving the oral cavity.

16. Which type of oral ulceration is most commonly caused by biting the inside of the cheek?

Correct Answer: 2. Biting the inside of the cheek commonly leads to traumatic ulceration.

17. Which of the following describes the characteristic appearance of oral lichen planus?

Correct Answer: 1. Oral lichen planus presents as white lacy patches in the mouth.

18. What is the first line of treatment for recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS)?

Correct Answer: 3. Topical corticosteroids are the first line of treatment for recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS).

19. Which autoimmune blistering disease presents with subepithelial blistering?

Correct Answer: 4. Mucous membrane pemphigoid is an autoimmune disease characterized by subepithelial blistering.

20. What is the common trigger for oral ulceration due to chemical trauma?

Correct Answer: 1. Oral ulceration due to chemical trauma can be triggered by placing aspirin directly on oral tissues.

21. Which medication is used in the management of mucous membrane pemphigoid?

Correct Answer: 3. Prednisolone is commonly used in the management of mucous membrane pemphigoid.

22. What type of virus is associated with oral ulceration in immunocompromised patients?

Correct Answer: 2. Herpes simplex virus (HSV) is associated with oral ulceration, especially in immunocompromised patients.

23. Which factor is commonly implicated in the pathogenesis of oral lichen planus?

Correct Answer: 4. Oral lichen planus is often due to a T-cell mediated immune response.

24. Which of the following best describes pemphigus vulgaris?

Correct Answer: 1. Pemphigus vulgaris is a life-threatening blistering disease characterized by intraepithelial blister formation.

25. What is a recommended management step for severe recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS)?

Correct Answer: 3. Severe cases of recurrent aphthous stomatitis may require immunomodulators such as colchicine for management.

26. What is the primary purpose of using systemic corticosteroids in managing pemphigus vulgaris?

Correct Answer: 2. Systemic corticosteroids are used in pemphigus vulgaris to suppress the autoimmune response that causes blister formation.

27. Which of the following conditions is characterized by a subepithelial blister?

Correct Answer: 4. Mucous membrane pemphigoid is characterized by subepithelial blister formation.

28. Which of the following is a common cause of physical trauma leading to oral ulceration?

Correct Answer: 1. Ill-fitting dentures are a common cause of physical trauma leading to oral ulceration.

29. Which oral ulceration is associated with a drug-induced thrombocytopenia?

Correct Answer: 3. Chemotherapy can cause oral ulceration and may lead to thrombocytopenia, resulting in petechiae.

30. What characterizes oral ulceration caused by radiotherapy?

Correct Answer: 2. Oral ulceration caused by radiotherapy is characterized by initial erythema, followed by sloughing and eventual diffuse superficial ulceration.

31. Which condition is associated with hemorrhagic crusting of the labial vermilion zone?

Correct Answer: 1. Erythema multiforme is associated with hemorrhagic crusting of the labial vermilion zone.

32. What is the first line of treatment for oral lichen planus?

Correct Answer: 3. The first line of treatment for oral lichen planus is topical corticosteroids.

33. Which virus is commonly associated with erythema multiforme?

Correct Answer: 2. Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV) is commonly associated with erythema multiforme.

34. Which medication is considered a third-line treatment for oral lichen planus?

Correct Answer: 4. Hydroxychloroquine is considered a third-line treatment for oral lichen planus.

35. What is a common feature of recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS)?

Correct Answer: 3. Recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS) is characterized by multiple shallow ulcers that are often painful.

36. Which of the following is commonly used in the management of erosive oral lichen planus?

Correct Answer: 2. Topical tacrolimus is used in the management of erosive oral lichen planus.

37. What is a potential cause of oral ulceration due to thermal trauma?

Correct Answer: 4. Oral ulceration due to thermal trauma can be caused by eating very hot foods, such as cheese in pizza.

38. What is a common clinical finding in pemphigus vulgaris?

Correct Answer: 3. Erosions in the buccal mucosa are a common clinical finding in pemphigus vulgaris.

39. Which type of oral ulceration is associated with vasculitis?

Correct Answer: 1. Behcet's disease is a type of vasculitis that can cause oral ulceration.

40. What is the recommended first-line treatment for severe erosive oral lichen planus?

Correct Answer: 2. The recommended first-line treatment for severe erosive oral lichen planus is topical corticosteroids along with intralesional corticosteroids.
